# Conclusions

## Overview

This analysis provides a structured understanding of gym member behavior, focusing on how customers interact with the facility and its services. The findings aim to support better decision-making for neighborhood sports clubs.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Customer Behavior is Heterogeneous

Members do not behave uniformly. There are clear differences in:
- Visit frequency
- Time spent in the gym
- Use of additional services

This suggests the existence of distinct customer segments with different needs and value levels.

---

### 2. Membership Type is a Key Differentiator

Differences between Standard and Premium members indicate that:
- Premium members tend to show higher engagement levels
- They are more likely to use additional services

This reinforces the importance of designing strategies to upgrade Standard members.

---

### 3. Service Usage is Not Evenly Distributed

Services such as:
- Group lessons
- Personal training
- Sauna
- Drink subscriptions

are used by specific subsets of customers, rather than the entire base.

This indicates opportunities for:
- Targeted marketing
- Better service bundling

---

### 4. Engagement is Linked to Service Adoption

Customers who use more services tend to:
- Visit more frequently
- Spend more time in the gym

This suggests that increasing service adoption may improve overall engagement.

---

## Business Recommendations

### 1. Promote High-Value Services

- Encourage adoption of personal training and group lessons
- Bundle services with Premium memberships

---

### 2. Segment Customers Strategically

Identify and target:
- Low-engagement users → activation campaigns
- High-engagement users → retention strategies
- Service-heavy users → loyalty programs

---

### 3. Optimize Membership Strategy

- Design clear incentives to upgrade from Standard to Premium
- Communicate the added value of Premium services effectively

---

### 4. Use Data for Decision-Making

Even small clubs can benefit from:
- Tracking customer behavior
- Monitoring service usage
- Adapting offerings based on real data

---

## Limitations

- No data on revenue or customer lifetime value
- No information on customer retention or cancellations
- Limited age range may not represent all gym users

---

## Final Thought

Data analysis can provide significant value even in small-scale sports clubs. By understanding their customers better, clubs can make smarter decisions, improve customer experience, and increase long-term sustainability.
