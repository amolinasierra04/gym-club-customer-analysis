# Business Questions

## Context

Neighborhood sports clubs operate with limited resources and need to maximize the value of their existing customer base. Understanding how members use the gym and which services they consume is essential to improve engagement, increase revenue, and optimize operations.

This project focuses on analyzing gym membership data to extract actionable insights that can support decision-making in small and medium-sized sports clubs.

---

## Main Objective

To understand customer behavior, identify key user segments, and detect opportunities to improve service adoption and customer engagement.

---

## Key Business Questions

### 1. Customer Profile
- What is the demographic profile of gym members?
- How is the customer base distributed by gender, age, and membership type?
- What does the “average member” look like?

---

### 2. Usage Behavior
- How frequently do members visit the gym?
- How long do they typically stay per session?
- Are there differences in usage patterns across different customer groups?

---

### 3. Membership Value
- Do Premium members use the gym more than Standard members?
- Are there behavioral differences that justify upgrading membership plans?

---

### 4. Service Adoption
- Which members attend group lessons?
- What type of customers are more likely to use personal training services?
- Who uses additional services such as sauna or drink subscriptions?

---

### 5. Customer Segmentation
- Can we identify distinct customer segments based on behavior and service usage?
- Are there identifiable groups such as high-engagement users, casual users, or service-heavy users?

---

### 6. Business Opportunities
- Which services show the highest potential for growth?
- What types of customers should be targeted for upselling (e.g., Premium plans, personal training)?
- How can clubs increase member engagement and retention using these insights?

---

## Expected Outcome

The goal is not only to describe the data, but to translate findings into practical recommendations that can help neighborhood sports clubs:
- Improve their service offering
- Increase customer satisfaction
- Optimize commercial strategies
